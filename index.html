WELCOME TEXT

UMBC's Information Systems (IS) blends computing, business, and data so students can design, build, and manage real information solutions. Our interdisciplinary approach, hands-on projects, and proximity to Maryland's tech hubs help you build in-demand skills for roles across IT and IS.

  • Programs: B.S. in Information Systems, minors & certificates, and accelerated BS/MS options.
  • Core skills: programming, databases, networks, systems analysis & design, analytics, and professional writing.
  • Careers: systems analyst, database developer, network manager, business/health IT, and more.

About Us

We are an interdisciplinary department focused on the study and application of information systems. Students engage with faculty, research centers, and industry partners to turn ideas into solutions. Explore majors, minors, certificates, and accelerated programs to tailor IS to your goals.

UMBC INFORMATION SYSTEMS AND TECHNOLOGY

PROGRAM DESCRIPTION

The B.S. in Information Systems gives you a strong technical and conceptual foundation across computing and business. You learn how information flows through organizations and how to create systems that improve decisions, processes, and experiences.

REQUIRED COURSES
  • IS 300 – Management Information Systems
  • IS 310 – Software and Hardware Concepts
  • IS 410 – Introduction to Database Design
  • IS 420 – Database Application Development
  • IS 425 – Decision Support Systems
  • IS 436 – Structured Systems Analysis & Design
  • IS 450 – Data Communications & Networks
  • IS 451 – Network Design & Management
  • Upper-Level IS Elective (3xx/4xx)
ELECTIVES

You will customize your degree with upper-level IS electives. Examples include:

  • IS 247 – Computer Programming II
  • IS 296 – Foundations of Data Science
  • IS 303 – Human-Centered Computing (HCI)
  • IS 427 – Artificial Intelligence
  • IS 470 – Software Testing
  • IS 202 – Systems Analysis Methods
  • IS 295 – Intermediate Business Applications
