UMBC Information Systems and Technology

Program Description

The Bachelor of Science in Information Systems provides a strong foundation in computing and business. Coursework spans programming, data and databases, networks, systems analysis and design, decision support, and technology management.

Required Courses (Major)

  • IS 300 – Management Information Systems (grade of B or higher)
  • IS 310 – Software and Hardware Concepts (grade of B or higher)
  • IS 410 – Introduction to Database Design
  • IS 420 – Database Application Development
  • IS 425 – Decision Support Systems
  • IS 436 – Structured Systems Analysis & Design
  • IS 450 – Data Communications and Networks
  • IS 451 – Network Design and Management
  • One upper-level IS elective (300/400 level)

Common supporting courses include MATH 155 (Elementary/Applied Calculus) and IS 147 (Introduction to Computer Programming).

Sample Upper-Level IS Electives

  • IS 303 – Fundamentals of Human-Computer Interaction
  • IS 403 – User Interface Design
  • IS 427 – Introduction to Artificial Intelligence
  • IS 428 – Data Mining Techniques and Applications
  • IS 430 – Information Systems and Security
  • IS 438 – Project Management
